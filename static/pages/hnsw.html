HNSW

The trick that makes semantic search fast when you have millions of docs.

try this

  1. The map shows every doc as a node. Lines are the "I'm a neighbor of yours" links HNSW built.
  2. Click layer 1 at the top of the graph — way fewer nodes. Higher layers are like express lanes; they let search skip across the map quickly.
  3. Type a query and click search & animate. Watch nodes light up in the order they were visited — the search hops down from the top layer to layer 0.
  4. Drag efSearch down to 2 and search again. Way fewer visits — but it might miss the best doc. Speed vs accuracy.
  5. Bump M up to 16, click rebuild. Denser graph, better recall, more memory.
why a layered graph?

If you checked every doc, semantic search would be slow with millions of docs. HNSW pre-builds a graph where each node knows its closest neighbors. To search, you start at the top (sparse, long-range links), greedily walk toward your query, then drop down a layer and repeat. You only check a few dozen nodes out of millions.
