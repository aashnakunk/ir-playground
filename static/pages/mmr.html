MMR diversity re-ranking

Pure relevance often returns near-duplicates. MMR penalizes that.

try this

  1. Run a search. The left column shows plain semantic order (relevance only).
  2. Drag the λ slider. λ=1 = pure relevance. λ=0 = pure diversity. Most useful zone is around 0.5–0.7.
  3. The right column shows the MMR-reordered list. Compare titles — MMR usually swaps in different angles on the topic.
  4. Scroll to "selection steps" to see, at each pick, the candidates considered, their relevance, and the diversity penalty.
Think of it like: building a playlist. Even if your top 5 songs are all by one artist, you'd want some variety. MMR is "great track, but how different is it from what I already picked?"
the MMR formula

MMR(d) = λ · sim(query, d) − (1−λ) · max sim(d, picked). We iteratively pick the doc with the highest MMR until we have top-k. The penalty is the doc's similarity to whichever already-picked doc it resembles most.
