Beam search

How LLMs pick their next word. Keep the top-k partial sentences, expand each, prune to top-k again.

try this

  1. Drag the beam width slider and re-run. Width 1 = greedy (just pick the most likely next word each step). Width 5 = explore 5 candidate sentences in parallel.
  2. Each step shows every beam being expanded, all candidates considered, then which were kept vs pruned.
  3. Notice: at width 1 you can get stuck on a locally good first word that leads to a bad sentence. Higher widths let you recover.
  4. The final winning sequence is highlighted at the top.
Think of it like: writing a sentence. Brainstorm 3 candidate next words. Continue each as its own draft. After a few words, pick the 3 best drafts, drop the rest. Repeat. You'd never get the best sentence by just always picking the most obvious next word.
where this shows up in modern AI

Every LLM uses some form of next-token sampling — beam search (or its variants like nucleus / top-p sampling) is the bookkeeping that decides which sequences to keep alive. Higher beam width = better text quality but slower generation. Production LLMs typically use beam width 1 (greedy) with temperature/top-p sampling for speed.
