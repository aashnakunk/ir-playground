try this

  1. Type a boolean expression with uppercase AND, OR, NOT and parens.
  2. Look at the parsed tokens and RPN (postfix) — that's what the engine actually evaluates.
  3. The trace shows each set-operation step (terms → AND/OR/NOT → final).
  4. Scroll to the inverted index: for every word, the list of doc-ids that contain it. This is the data structure that makes keyword search fast.
  5. Click a term in the index to insert it into the query.
Think of it like: Spotify filters. rock AND NOT live, jazz OR latin. No "best match" — songs either pass the filter or don't. Old-school, but still the right tool when you need hard rules.
what's the inverted index doing?

Instead of scanning every doc for every query, we precompute a map: word → list of doc-ids. Now a AND b is just a set intersection of two short lists. BM25 and TF-IDF use the same structure under the hood — they just add scoring on top of these postings.
